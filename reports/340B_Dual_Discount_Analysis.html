The Oversight Gap: High-Spend States Without Carve-In Tracking

States with large Medicaid drug programs but zero carve-in contract pharmacy arrangements may be using carve-out approaches (excluding 340B from Medicaid), or may lack visibility into whether dual discounts are occurring.

Oversight concern: Virginia ($6.1B), Ohio ($4.8B), and Pennsylvania ($4.6B) each have thousands of 340B entities and billions in Medicaid drug spending, yet show zero carve-in contract pharmacy arrangements. While these states may use carve-out at the entity level, the GAO has noted that carve-out enforcement is inconsistent, and the absence of carve-in tracking does not guarantee dual discounts are being prevented.

Analysis Methodology & Data Sources

This analysis joins three public datasets: (1) HRSA 340B OPAIS Covered Entity Export (March 2026) — entity registrations and contract pharmacy arrangements with Medicaid billing flags; (2) CMS State Drug Utilization Data (2023, updated Dec 2025) — quarterly Medicaid prescription drug claims by state and NDC; (3) CMS Medicaid Spending by Drug (2023) — national drug-level spending trends. The key linkage is the "Medicaid Billing" field in contract pharmacy data, which identifies carve-in arrangements where 340B drugs are billed to Medicaid — the condition under which duplicate discounts can occur. State-level exposure is estimated by overlaying carve-in activity density onto Medicaid drug spending volume.
